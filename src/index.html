Burlington County Highway Master Plan    |     Project Overview

  •  About
  •  Contact
  •  Disclaimer

Burlington County has grown by more than 50,000 residents and 25,000 jobs since the County last prepared a Highway Master Plan in 1989. An almost equal number of people and jobs are forecasted to be added by the Year 2040. The Delaware Valley Regional Planning Commission (DVRPC) was commissioned to update the Highway Master Plan to supply a vision and implementation framework for the County highway network that can sustainably complement long-term County growth patterns.

The County Route (CR) network is vital to everday movement in the County, and it plays an important role in interconnecting with the New Jersey Turnpike and interstate highway system. The CR Network carries NJ Transit and BurLink bus routes, provides access to stations along the NJ Transit RiverLINE, and serves as a scenic route network in the Pinelands.

Performance indicators for the road network, as well as land use conditions, were assembled for current and forecasted conditions. The conditions were assessed using DVRPC's Traffic Management Process (TMP) to identify candidate improvement strategies for the County road network.

This webmap provides an interactive look at the information that informed the analysis and the resulting improvement strategies. The base year for the analysis was 2013, while 2040 was used as the forecast year when necessary.

Burlington County Highway Master Plan    |     Strategies

  •  About
  •  Data Dictionary

DVRPC conducts a continuing, comprehensive and cooperative process for transportation planning, programming, and decision-making. Two important outcomes of this process are the Long-Range Plan (LRP) and Transportation Improvement Program (TIP). The Traffic Management Process (TMP) provides medium-term planning to strengthen the connection between the Long-Range Plan (LRP) and the regional Transportation Improvement Program (TIP).

Federal regulations require that alternatives to building new Single Occupancy Vehicle (SOV) road capacity be explored. Even where new capacity is found to be appropriate and necessary, multimodal supplemental strategies to get the most long-term value from the investment must be included. The CMP identifies these multimodal strategies for congested corridors, and projects that add SOV capacity must be consistent with the CMP in order to be eligible for federal funding.

Inventorying current plans, programs, and pipelined projects is an important part of identifying candidate improvement strategies for the County's highway infrastructure. The components below include physical improvements related to the plans and programs that will address traffic safety and affect mobility. Projects that will deliver mobility changes were included in the Year 2040 LRP scenario to estimate future traffic conditions.

Burlington County Highway Master Plan    |     Right-of-Way

  •  About
  •  Data Dictionary

An important element of the Burlington County Highway Master Plan is the updated map of proposed right-of-way (ROW) widths. The proposed ROW widths are to be used as a planning tool in designing roadway improvements and evaluating land development applications. It is very important for the county to reserve additional ROW because land development projects can have an impact on traffic circulation on adjacent county roads.


Proposed Right-of-Way

A proposed ROW width has been assigned to each County Route (CR) segment based on its assigned functional classification and urban/rural area boundaries (USDOT Federal Highway Administration's Highway Functional Classification System (HFCS)).

Capacity Assessment

An analysis was conducted to determine the volume-to-capacity (V/C) ratio of each CR segment. V/C ratio represents the ability of a roadway to accommodate (capacity) vehicular demand (volume), and it is a measure of traffic congestion. As the V/C ratio approaches 1, vehicles may experience delay or queuing. Therefore, a V/C ratio greater than 0.85 indicates that there may be a potential need to increase roadway capacity in the future.

Design Elements by ROW Category

The accompanying cross section diagram (click the link in the pop-up window to view the diagram) for each ROW category details the recommended width for each roadway design element. It is important to note that these cross section smay not exactly reflect existing or future conditions.
